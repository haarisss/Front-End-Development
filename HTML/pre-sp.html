Secondary school life

When I was previously pursuing GCE O Levels in Ngee Ann Secondary School,there was always much competition as expected. Throughout my 4 years, it has always been a challenge to top the class, forget about topping the cohort. There was always a lot of pressure and mostly due to the good reputation of the school that they want to continue upholding. It's not a surprise as whenever I infom someone that I'm in Ngee Ann, they seemed impressed and complimented me for being in a good school. When i was in lower secondary, classes were always fun especially when I'm working with my friends. We had many funny bonding memories. Before heading to upper secondary, I chose the triple science stream as my subject combination for GCE O Levels. Classes were dull most of the times but fun sometimes which is not a surprise because we were closer to O Levels and thus classes were more serious.Life was even harder in upper secondary due to my subject combiantion which requires a lot of commitment and there were multiple times when i felt like giving up but I believed in myself, persisted and eventually scored decently in O Levels. I took part in many academic competitions throughout the 4 years such as Singapore Mathematical Olympiad, Singapore Polytechnic Debate Open as well as Singapore Junior Chemistry Olympiad. I really enjoyed camps and my favourite was the Advanced Leadership Training Camp.

CCA

I was in Photography Club in secondary School and the only reason I chose it was because it was only once a week andI can just relax most of the time. Soon, I started liking photography and I enjoyed cca sessions to the extent that I wished CCA was twice a week. CCA was really fun because of the members in the CCA who are very friendly. I also has a good relationship with the teachers in charge. I made a lot of friends in CCA. In secondary 3 , I was elected the Vice President of Photography Club which is why I went for the leadership camp and I loved it not only because it was at Sentosa but because it was really fun and enriching. Being vice president was fun and tiring, I had to come up with CCA plans and also ensure the members are doing what they are supposed to. I got scolded a lot but I also had a really good time. I took part in many photography competitions and I was also one of the trainers for Monochrome Photography for the Singapore Young Photographers Convention at Anglo Chinese Secondary International.
